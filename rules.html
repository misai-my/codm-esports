General

Rule Acceptance

By registering, checking in, joining a match lobby, or participating in an official series, teams and players confirm that they understand and agree to the tournament rules, admin rulings, match procedures, and conduct expectations.

  • Admins may issue final rulings for cases not clearly covered by the rulebook.
  • Rule updates may be announced through the tournament platform or official Discord.
  • Teams are responsible for reading updates before their scheduled matches.
Registration

Team & Player Eligibility

  • Each team must submit an official roster with five main players and allowed substitutes.
  • Players may not register under multiple teams in the same tournament.
  • Captain contact details must be accurate for match coordination and admin rulings.
  • Admins may request verification for player identity, Discord account, or CODM UID.

Map Veto

Veto Procedure

  • Teams must join the assigned Discord match channel when called by admin.
  • Captains must confirm attendance in the veto room.
  • Admin selects which captain calls heads or tails for the coin toss.
  • The coin toss winner chooses Team 1 or Team 2. Team 1 starts veto selection.
  • Each map ban, map pick, or side selection has a visible countdown timer.
  • If a team becomes unresponsive, admin may randomize the current action.
  • First call is final once locked in the veto room.

Results

Match Reporting & Disputes

  • Captains must submit score reports through the My Matches page when required.
  • Admins review reports before official scores are locked.
  • Disputes must include clear evidence such as screenshots, VODs, room results, or admin logs.
  • Late, incomplete, or abusive disputes may be rejected.
Integrity

Conduct & Penalties

  • Teams must maintain professional conduct toward opponents, admins, and broadcast staff.
  • Cheating, account sharing, exploit abuse, ghosting, or intentional disconnection may lead to penalties.
  • Penalties may include warnings, map loss, match loss, roster disqualification, or tournament removal.
  • Admin decisions are final for live tournament operations.
